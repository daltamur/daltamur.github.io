The Forest for the Tries

Posted on April 13, 2021 at 6:46 PM

   So, my laptop of almost 6 years finally gave out a month ago now. I was in a pinch and couldn't really afford to wait and maybe fix it by just getting an SSD so I gave in and bought a Thinkpad. Purists have always told me Thinkpads are the best laptops for programmers, but I would have never fathomed how great they are for everyday use and are an affordable, reasonably sized choice for anyone.

First, we obviously have to talk price and in terms of cost, this is the most bang-for-your-buck laptop I've ever used. I got a 10th gen i7 CPU, 1 TB SSD, and 16 GB of RAM for all just over $800. Sure, there's specs about these specs we could go into, but they allow for maximum efficiency and are top of the line specs for any laptop.

Next, we have to talk about the look. I'll be first to admit that the aesthetics of a Thinkpad aren't especially sleek like a Macbook.

Even the promotional images can't make it look not just like a boring black slab.

They really are designed primarily as business machines. No sleek or colorful bodies here, it's a no frills black box. But that's the beauty of it, they don't throw things in the machine that you're not going to use. Take tha Macbook for instance, a comparable model to the Thinkpad is nearly $2500 while a Thinkpad with the exact same specs is more than $1000 less. It's because of this that I really think the Thinkpad brand is for everyone. We're in an age where laptops are a necessity and something as powerful as this at less than $1000 is some serious value that I think everyone is after. And hey, you can always get a case to doll it's black exterior up.
